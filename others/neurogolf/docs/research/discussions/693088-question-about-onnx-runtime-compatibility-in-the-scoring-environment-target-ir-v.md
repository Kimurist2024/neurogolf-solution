## Body

Dear Host ,
Could you kindly clarify the exact ONNX compatibility stack used in the competition scoring environment?

I’m specifically trying to confirm:
- the `onnx` version
- the `onnxruntime` version
- the maximum supported ONNX `ir_version`
- the supported ONNX `opset` range used during validation/scoring

I’m seeing some models fail even though they appear structurally valid in newer local environments. For example, a few models exported as `IR 13 / opset 12` fail to load in my local validation path, so I want to confirm the official competition limits rather than guess.

I understand that the following ONNX features/operations are disallowed: `Loop`, `Scan`, `NonZero`, `Unique`, `Script`, and `Function`. However, it also seems that some other operators may not work reliably in the actual scoring/runtime environment even if they are not explicitly banned. For example, I suspect `TopK` and possibly some others may fail or behave inconsistently in practice.

Could you clarify whether there is a broader list of operators that are unsupported in the real evaluation environment, beyond the explicitly disallowed set?

For example, one of my local environments is using:

onnx: 1.18.0
onnxruntime: 1.22.0

In that environment:

onnx.checker only supports up to IR version 11
local onnxruntime only supports up to IR version 10

But my another environment exported the model with IR 13 / opset 12.
and Kaggle env : py=3.12.12|onnx=1.20.1|ort=1.24.4|onnx_tool=unknown

So an older local stack may fail to load an ONNX model generated by a newer stack, because the model format is too new for the older runtime.

**A precise compatibility list would help avoid submission failures caused by exporter/runtime mismatches.**

Thanks.
